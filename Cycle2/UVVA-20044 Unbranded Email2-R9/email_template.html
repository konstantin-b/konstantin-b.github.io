CONSIDER HOW

PAINFUL VVA CAN BE

There are many names for vulvar and vaginal atrophy (VVA). Whether it’s referred to as atrophic vaginitis, vaginal atrophy, or part of the genitourinary syndrome of menopause, the outcome is the same: it’s a painful condition that affects nearly 1 in 2 menopausal women in the United States.1


That means that approximately half of your menopausal patients may be experiencing unpleasant symptoms that may not resolve without treatment. In fact, in a survey of menopausal women:

81% were unaware that VVA is a medical condition2

55% had vaginal dryness—the most common symptom of VVA1

44% reported dyspareunia, another common symptom of VVA1

77% experienced vaginal itching or irritation2
