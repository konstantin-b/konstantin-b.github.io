Compliance WITH TREATMENT is
critical for PATIENTS WITH VVA

Did you know that only 7% of menopausal women with vulvar and vaginal atrophy (VVA) are treated with prescription therapy annually1,2 and that they refill an average of only 2.4 prescriptions per year?3


Common local vaginal estrogen treatment options include1:

Creams

Inserts

Rings

For many patients, compliance barriers, such as dissatisfaction with their treatment, may lead to treatment discontinuationâ€”which can trigger the return of painful symptoms. To enhance compliance, be sure to discuss the risks and benefits of all appropriate treatment options with your patients.1


VVA is a chronic progressive condition so it is important for women to stay on treatment for an appropriate length of therapy. When treatment is interrupted, it may cause the symptoms to return. It is important to periodically discuss with your patients whether treatment is still necessary.
