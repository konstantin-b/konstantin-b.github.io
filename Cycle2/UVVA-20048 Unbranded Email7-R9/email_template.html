The emotional impact

Of vva

Only half of women talk to their healthcare providers about their symptoms of vulvar and vaginal atrophy (VVA) due to menopause. This is what surveys tell us about menopausal women and VVA:

About one third reported that VVA symptoms had a negative impact on self-esteem (34%) or mood (42%)1

About half reported that their symptoms had a negative impact on their relationship with their partner (47%) or on intimacy and their relationship with their partner (55%)1,2

More than half (62%) reported lower confidence due to vaginal dryness and discomfort3

The conversations with your patients don't have to be painful. Only 56% of menopausal women with VVA surveyed discussed VVA symptoms with their HCPs—which means you might never know the true impact of VVA on your patients unless you bring it up first.2 Asking just one more question could change everything.
